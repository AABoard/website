What is AAB?

The International AI Assessment Board (AAB) is a nonprofit initiative dedicated to advancing AI literacy and AI education standards across K–12 education, adult learning, and workforce AI training.

AAB maintains an open AI education case registry and pilot registry to document real‑world AI learning programs and support evidence‑based AI education research.

Our work is independent, transparent, and public‑interest driven.

Research-Based Case Collection

AAB collects AI education cases from published academic papers, research projects, and empirical studies. These research-based cases provide documented evidence of how AI is being used in education across different learning contexts.

Cases may originate from peer-reviewed journal articles, conference papers, institutional research reports, funded research projects, and large-scale educational studies.

  • Peer-reviewed journal papers
  • Conference research publications
  • Funded research projects
  • Institutional research studies
  • Published evaluation reports

This research-based evidence complements practice-based cases collected through regional pilots and institutional collaborations.

Practice-Based Case Collection

Regional liaisons and partner institutions also contribute real-world AI education pilots including school programs, community initiatives, and workforce training pilots.

Role of the Academic Advisory Board

The Academic Advisory Board provides occasional strategic guidance, academic perspective, and external credibility for the development of AAB and its Global AI Education Case Registry.

  • Offer light-touch academic guidance on AAB mission and direction
  • Provide perspective on emerging AI education research and practice
  • Strengthen the visibility and credibility of the initiative
  • Support connections across institutions, disciplines, and regions
