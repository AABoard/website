Who Will Define AI Literacy? The International AI Assessment Board (AAB) Launches a Global Initiative for Standards, Assessment, and Case-Based Evidence

Published March 17, 2025
As artificial intelligence rapidly reshapes education and workforce development, AAB launches a global initiative to build AI literacy standards, assessment frameworks, and a case-based evidence system.

As artificial intelligence rapidly transforms education, workforce development, and society at large, a fundamental question remains unresolved: Who defines AI literacy?

Today, the International AI Assessment Board (AAB) announces its launch as a global initiative dedicated to establishing AI literacy standards, assessment frameworks, and a case-based evidence system for education.

Despite the rapid proliferation of AI courses, tools, and programs worldwide, the field remains highly fragmented. Educators, institutions, and policymakers face a growing challenge: there is no widely accepted framework to define, measure, or validate AI literacy across K–12, higher education, and workforce contexts.

AAB is founded to address this critical gap.

A New Layer of Infrastructure for AI Education

AAB positions AI literacy not as a collection of tools or courses, but as a form of foundational human infrastructure that requires:

  • Clear and adaptable standards
  • Rigorous and transparent assessment
  • Real-world, comparable evidence

At the core of AAB’s approach is a case-based evidence system, designed to systematically collect, structure, and analyze global AI education practices—from research studies to real-world pilots.

A Three-Part Mission

  1. AI Literacy Standards — Developing a structured framework that defines AI literacy across age groups, disciplines, and application contexts.
  2. Assessment & Validation — Designing methodologies to evaluate AI learning outcomes beyond traditional testing, including project-based and applied competencies.
  3. Global Case Registry — Building an open, structured repository of AI education cases and pilot programs to support evidence-based decision-making.

A Collaborative Global Effort

AAB is designed as an independent, nonprofit-oriented initiative working in collaboration with academic institutions, educators, researchers, schools, education systems, and industry partners.

Its development is informed by global conversations around AI education, including alignment with emerging international frameworks and policy discussions.

Ecosystem Structure

  • AAB — Standards, assessment, and evidence
  • AEA — Community, collaboration, and outreach
  • Implementation partners — Programs, platforms, and services

This structure supports both independence in standard-setting and scalability in real-world adoption.

Call for Participation

AAB invites academics, educators, researchers, institutions, and organizations to participate in its early-stage development.

Opportunities include:

  • Contributing cases to the global registry
  • Providing feedback on draft frameworks
  • Joining the Academic Advisory Board

A Defining Moment

As AI becomes a universal layer across education and work, the need for shared definitions, trusted assessment, and evidence-based frameworks is becoming urgent.

AAB aims to help shape that foundation.
